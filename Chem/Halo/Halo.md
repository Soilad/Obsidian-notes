### Introduction:
 - **Alkyl:** H attached to $sp^3$ carbon on group (RX)
 - **Aryl:** H attached to $sp^2$ carbon on group (ArX)
 - **Applications:**
	 1. non-polar compound solvent
	 2. starting material for organic synthesis
	 3. medicines (chloramphenicol, chloroquine, halothane)
	 4. hormones (thyroxine)
	 5. blood substitues
	- **Enviromental Concerns:** Halo Compounds pesist due to resistance to breakdown
### Classification
- **Based on Number of Halogen Atoms:**
    - **Mono-, Di-, Polyhalogen:** (Tri-, Tetra-, etc.)
        
- **Compounds Containing sp³ C-X Bond:**
    - **(a) Alkyl Halides (R-X):**
        - **Primary (1°):** Halogen on primary carbon.
            
        - **Secondary (2°):** Halogen on secondary carbon.
            
        - **Tertiary (3°):** Halogen on tertiary carbon.
            
    - **(b) Allylic Halides:** Halogen on sp³ carbon adjacent to C=C.
    - **(c) Benzylic Halides:** Halogen on sp³ carbon next to aromatic ring.
        
- **Compounds Containing sp² C-X Bond:**
    - **(a) [[Vinyl]] Halides:** Halogen on sp² carbon of C=C.
        
    - **(b) Aryl Halides:** Halogen directly on sp² carbon of aromatic ring.

- **Dihaloalkanes:**
	- **Geminal Halides (gem-dihalides):** Both halogens on the same carbon (e.g., ethylidene chloride).
	- **Vicinal Halides (vic-dihalides):** Halogens on adjacent carbons (e.g., ethylene dichloride).
### Nature of C-X Bond
- **Polarity:** Halogen atoms are more electronegative than carbon, so C-X bond is polarized (Cδ⁺-Xδ⁻).
    
- **Bond Length:** Increases down the group (C-F < C-Cl < C-Br < C-I) as halogen size increases.
    
- **Bond Enthalpy:** Decreases down the group (C-F > C-Cl > C-Br > C-I).
   
- **Dipole Moment:** Generally high, but not directly proportional to electronegativity difference due to molecular geometry.
### Physical Properties
- **Color:** Pure alkyl halides are colorless; bromides and iodides develop color on light exposure.

- **Odor:** Many volatile halogen compounds have sweet smell.

- **Melting and Boiling Points:**
    - Higher than parent hydrocarbons (due to greater polarity and molecular mass, stronger dipole-dipole and van der Waals forces).
        
    - **Order for same alkyl group:** RI > RBr > RCl > RF (due to increasing size/mass of halogen, increasing van der Waals forces).
        
    - **Branching:** Boiling points of isomeric haloalkanes decrease with increasing branching.
        
    - **Dihalobenzenes:** Isomeric dihalobenzenes have similar boiling points. Para-isomers have higher melting points (better crystal lattice fit due to symmetry).
        
- **Density:** Bromo, iodo, and polychloro derivatives are heavier than water. Increases with number of C atoms, halogen atoms, and atomic mass of halogen.
    
- **Solubility:**
    - Very slightly soluble in water (energy required to break H-bonds in water > energy released by new haloalkane-water attractions).
        
    - Soluble in organic solvents (intermolecular attractions are similar).
