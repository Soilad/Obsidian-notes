#reactions/preparation 
### Haloalkanes
1. **Alcohols:**
	> not applicable to aryl halide (CO partial double bond => hard to break)
	
	- $\ce{ROH ->[PX3, PCl5, SOCl2] RX}$
	- $\ce{ROH ->[SOCl2] RX + \uparrow SO2_{(g)} + \uparrow HCl_{(g)}}$ (Preferred)
	- [[Lucas Test]]
	- $\ce{ROH ->[\Delta][48\% HBr] RBr}$
	- $\ce{ROH ->[\Delta, HI][ NaI/KI + H3PO4] RBr}$
2. **Hydrocarbons:**
	- Alkanes
			$\ce{RCH3 ->[Cl2/Br2][uv/\Delta] RCH2(Cl/Br)}$
	- Alkenes
		- [[Markonikov's Rule]]
		- $\ce{RCH3=CH3R' ->[Br2][CCl4] RCH2Br=CH2BrR'_{(vicinal)}}$ (detects C=C)
3. **Halogen Exchange:**
	- Finkelstein Reaction:
			$\ce{R(Cl/Br) + NaI ->[Dry acetone] RI + \downarrow Na(Cl/Br)_{(s)}}$
	- Swartz Reaction:
			$\ce{R(Cl/Br) ->[\Delta][AgF/HgF2/CoF2/SbF3] RF}$
### Haloarenes
> Forms o- and p- compounds
1. **Hydrocarbons:**
	$\ce{Ar-H + (Cl2/Br2/I2) ->[Fe/FeX3][in dark] Ar-(Cl2/Br2/I2)}$
	$\ce{Ar-I->[HNO3/HIO4] Ar-H}$
	$\ce{Ar-H + F2} \not \rightarrow (F_2\ too\ reactive)$ 
2. **Diazo:**
	- [[Sandmeyer]]
	- [[Gattermann]]
