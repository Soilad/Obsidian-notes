### Introduction
- **Alcohol** -OH group attached to an sp³ hybridized carbon of an aliphatic system (R-OH).
- **Phenol:** -OH group directly attached to an sp² hybridized carbon of an aromatic system (Ar-OH).
### Classification
- **Alcohols & Phenols:**
	- **Monohydric (1 -OH group):**
	    - **Csp³-OH bond:**
	        - **Primary (1°):** -OH on primary carbon (-CH₂OH).
	        - **Secondary (2°):** -OH on secondary carbon (>CHOH).
	        - **Tertiary (3°):** -OH on tertiary carbon (>COH).
	        - **Allylic:** -OH on sp³ carbon adjacent to C=C (e.g., CH₂=CH-CH₂OH).
	        - **Benzylic:** -OH on sp³ carbon next to aromatic ring (e.g., C₆H₅-CH₂OH).
	    - **Csp²-OH bond:**
	        - **Vinylic:** -OH on sp² carbon of C=C (e.g., CH₂=CH-OH).
	        - **Aryl:** -OH on sp² carbon of aromatic ring (Phenols).
	- **Dihydric:** 2 -OH groups.
	- **Trihydric/Polyhydric:** Three or more -OH groups.
### Structure:
- **Alcohols:** O of -OH attached to C by σ bond (sp³-sp³ overlap). Bond angle slightly less than tetrahedral (109.5°) due to lone pair repulsion.
- **Phenols:** -OH attached to sp² hybridized carbon. C-O bond length (136 pm) shorter than in methanol (142 pm) due to partial double bond character (resonance) and sp² hybridization of carbon.
### Physical Properties
- **Boiling Points:**
    - Increase with increasing carbon atoms (van der Waals forces).
    - Decrease with branching (decreased surface area, weaker van der Waals forces).
    - Higher than hydrocarbons, ethers, haloalkanes of comparable molecular mass due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding.
        
- **Solubility:**
    - Soluble in water due to H-bonding with water molecules.
    - Solubility decreases with increasing size of hydrophobic alkyl/aryl groups.
    - Lower molecular mass alcohols are miscible with water.
	
- **Acidity of Alcohols:**
    - Due to polar O-H bond.
    - Electron-releasing groups (-CH₃, -C₂H₅) decrease acidity (increase electron density on O, decrease O-H polarity).
    - **Order:** Primary > Secondary > Tertiary.
    - Weaker acids than water (alkoxides are stronger bases than hydroxides).
    - Act as Brønsted bases due to lone pair on oxygen (proton acceptors).
        
- **Acidity of Phenols:**
    - Stronger acids than alcohols and water.
        
    - -OH attached to sp² carbon (electron-withdrawing).
        
    - **[[Aryl Resonance]]:** Phenoxide ion is more stable than phenol due to delocalization of negative charge over the ring (five resonating structures for phenoxide, but charge separation in phenol).
		
        
    - **Substituted Phenols:**
        - Electron-withdrawing groups (-NO₂) enhance acidity (especially at o/p positions) by stabilizing phenoxide ion.
            
        - Electron-releasing groups (alkyl groups) decrease acidity (e.g., cresols are less acidic than phenol).
            
    - **pKₐ:** Smaller pKₐ means stronger acid. Phenol (pKₐ 10.0) is much more acidic than ethanol (pKₐ 15.9).
