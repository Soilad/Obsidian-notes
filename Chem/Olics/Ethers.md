### Introduction:
- Oxygen atom bonded to two alkyl/aryl groups (R-O-R', Ar-O-R, Ar-O-Ar'). Can be viewed as H of -OH in alcohol/phenol replaced by R/Ar.
### Classification
- **Simple/Symmetrical:** Alkyl/aryl groups attached to oxygen are the same (e.g., diethyl ether).
- **Mixed/Unsymmetrical:** Alkyl/aryl groups are different (e.g., ethyl methyl ether).
### Structure:
- Four electron pairs on oxygen (two bond, two lone) in tetrahedral arrangement. Bond angle slightly greater than tetrahedral due to repulsion between bulky R groups. C-O bond length (141 pm) similar to alcohols.
### Physical Properties
- **Polarity:** C-O bonds are polar, so ethers have a net dipole moment.
- **Boiling Points:**
    - Comparable to alkanes of similar molecular mass.
        
    - Much lower than alcohols of similar molecular mass (due to lack of H-bonding in ethers).
        
- **Solubility:**
    - Miscible with water to a similar extent as alcohols of same molecular mass (oxygen can form H-bonds with water).
