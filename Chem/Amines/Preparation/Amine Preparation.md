#reactions/preparation
1. **Reduction of Nitro Compounds:**
	 $\ce{R - NO_2 ->[Sn/HCl or Fe/HCl] R-NH_2}$
    - **Reagents:** H₂/Pd (ethanol), Sn/HCl, or Fe/HCl ( cheapest method ).
    - **Note:** Fe/HCl is preferred as FeCl₂ hydrolyzes to regenerate HCl, requiring less initial acid.
        
2. **Ammonolysis of [[Halo]] Compounds:**
	$\ce{Alc:NH_3 + R-X ->[373K] R-N^+H_3X^- + NH_3 <=> R-NH_2 + NH_4X}$
    - **Process:** Nucleophilic substitution of halogen by -NH₂ group using ethanolic ammonia. (ammonia in ethanol)
        
    - **Disadvantage:** Yields a mixture of 1°, 2°, 3° amines and quaternary ammonium salt.
        
    - **Control:** Large excess of ammonia favors primary amine formation.
        
    - **Reactivity Order:** RI > RBr > RCl.
        
    - **Free Amine:** Obtained from ammonium salt by treatment with a strong base (e.g., NaOH).

			$\ce{R-C \equiv N ->[LiAlH_4 or H_2/Ni] R-CH_2NH_2}$
	    - **Ascent of Amine Series:** Increases carbon chain by one.
