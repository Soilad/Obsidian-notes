### Classification of Amines
- **Primary (1°):** One H atom of NH₃ replaced (RNH₂ or ArNH₂).
- **Secondary (2°):** Two H atoms of NH₃ replaced (R₂NH or RNHR').
- **Tertiary (3°):** All three H atoms of NH₃ replaced (R₃N or RNR'R'').
- **Simple Amines:** All alkyl/aryl groups are the same.
- **Mixed Amines:** Alkyl/aryl groups are different.> **Diazonium salts:** Important intermediates for synthesizing aromatic compounds and azo dyes.
### Physical Properties
- **Lower Aliphatic Amines:** Gases with fishy odor.
    
- **Primary Amines (3+ carbons):** Liquids; higher ones are solids.
    
- **Aniline/Arylamines:** Colorless, but color on storage due to atmospheric oxidation.
    
- **Solubility:**
    - Lower aliphatic amines are soluble in water (H-bonding with water).
        
    - Solubility decreases with increasing molar mass (larger hydrophobic alkyl part).
        
    - Higher amines are insoluble in water.
        
    - Soluble in organic solvents (alcohol, ether, benzene).
        
- **Boiling Points:**
    - Alcohols are more polar and form stronger H-bonds than amines.
        
    - Primary and secondary amines show intermolecular association due to H-bonding.
        
    - Primary amines have stronger intermolecular association (two H atoms for H-bonding).
        
    - Tertiary amines lack H atoms for H-bonding, so no intermolecular association.
        
    - **Order of Boiling Points (isomeric amines):** Primary > Secondary > Tertiary
