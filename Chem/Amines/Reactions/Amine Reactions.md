#reactions
1. Basic due to unshared electron pair of nitrogen (Lewis Base)
2. $\ce{R-NH_{2} + HX <=> R-NH^+_{3}X_^-}$
3. Soluble in water
4. $\ce{R-NH_{3}^{+}X^- +NaOH -> R-NH_{2}}$
5. Stronger bases due to +[[Inductive Effect]] of alkyl grp (increases $e^-$ density on N)
6. Aryl amine < Alkyl amines due to $e^-$ withdrawing nature of aryl group
7. **Substituted Anilines**:
	- $e^-$ releasing groups ($-OCH_{3}-$, $-CH_{3}$)  $\uparrow$ basic strength
	- $e^-$ withdrawing groups ($-NO_{2}, -SO_{3}H, -COOH$ )  $\downarrow$ basic strength
8. Basicity:
	- **in Gaseous Phase:** Tertiary > Secondary > Primary > NH₃ (due to [[Inductive Effect]]).
	- **Aqueous Phase:** Complex interplay of [[Inductive Effect]], Solvation Effect, and [[Steric Hindrance]].
		- **Methyl-substituted:** (CH₃)₂NH > CH₃NH₂ > (CH₃)₃N > NH₃
		- **Ethyl-substituted:** (C₂H₅)₂NH > (C₂H₅)₃N > C₂H₅NH₂ > NH₃
		- **Solvation Effect:** Greater stability of substituted ammonium cation by solvation leads to stronger base. Primary > Secondary > Tertiary for stability of solvated ions.
		- **[[Steric Hindrance]]:** Bigger alkyl groups hinder H-bonding, affecting basic strength.
	
**Alkylation**:
	$\ce{R_{1}-NH-R_{2} + R_{3}-X \to R_{1}-(N-R_{2})-R_{3} + HX}$
	
**Acylation**:
	$\ce{2R_{1}-NH_{2} + R_{2}-OOX <=> R_{1}-NHOO-R_{2} + R_{1}-NH_{3}^+X^-}$
- **Reactants:** 1° and 2° amines + acid chlorides, anhydrides, or esters.
- **Mechanism:** Nucleophilic substitution, replacement of H atom of -NH₂ or >N-H by acyl group.
- **Products:** Amides.
- **Catalyst:** Base stronger than amine (e.g., pyridine) to remove HCl and shift equilibrium.
- **Benzoylation:** Reaction with benzoyl chloride (C₆H₅COCl).
- **Carboxylic Acids:** Form salts with amines at room temperature, not amides directly.
- **Acetanilide:** Lone pair on N is less available for donation to benzene ring due to resonance with carbonyl oxygen, reducing activating effect.

**Carbylamine reaction**:
$\ce{R-NH_{3} + CHCl3 + Alc KOH ->[\Delta] R-N\equiv C}$
- **Products:** Isocyanides (carbylamines), foul-smelling substances.
- **Distinction:** Only 1° amines show this reaction; 2° and 3° amines do not.

**Nitrous Acid Reaction**:
> $\ce{NaNO2 + HCl -> HNO2}$
- **Primary Aliphatic Amines**:
	$\ce{R-NH2 + HNO2 -> [R-N2Cl] ->[H2O] R-OH +N2 + HCl}$
- **Aromatic Amines**:
	$\ce{C6H5-NH2 + NaNO2 + 2HCl -> C6H5-N2^{+}Cl^{-} + NaCl + 2H2O}$

**Reaction with Arylsulphonyl Chloride (Hinsberg's Reagent)**:

**Primary Amine**
[[Hinsberg Reagent]]$\ce{ + R-NH2 ->}$
```smiles
c1ccccc1S(=O)(=O)N[R]
```
H on N is strongly acidic due to $e^-$ withdrawing of sulphonyl group

**Secondary Amine**
[[Hinsberg Reagent]]$\ce{ + R-NH-R ->}$
```smiles
c1ccccc1S(=O)(=O)N(-[R])(-[R])
```
no H atom present $\therefore$ not acidic
