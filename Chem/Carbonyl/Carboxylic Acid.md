### Structure of Carboxyl Group:
- **Geometry:** Bonds to carboxyl carbon are coplanar, ~120° angles.

- **Electrophilicity:** Carboxyl carbon is less electrophilic than carbonyl carbon (due to resonance with hydroxyl oxygen).
	        
### Carbonyl Group:
- Bonded to 1 R and 1 OH
```smiles
[R]C(=O)O
```
- **Derivatives:**
**Amides**
```smiles
NC(=O)[R]
```

**Acyl Halides:**
```smiles
[X]C(=O)[R]
```
**Esters:**
```smiles
[R]C(=O)O[R]
```
**Anhydrides:**
```smiles
[R]C(=O)OC(=O)[R]
```
**Natural Occurrence:**
- Fatty acids (C₁₂-C₁₈) in natural fats.
### Uses:
- materials (adhesives, plastics, perfumes)
- Starting material for anhydrides, esters, acid chlorides, amides.
- **Methanoic acid:** Rubber, textile, dyeing, leather, electroplating.
- **Ethanoic acid:** Solvent, vinegar.
- **Hexanedioic acid:** Nylon-6,6 manufacture.
- **Benzoic acid esters:** Perfumery.
- **Sodium benzoate:** Food preservative.
- **Higher fatty acids:** Soaps and detergents.
### Physical Properties
- **State:** Aliphatic (up to 9 carbons) are colorless liquids with unpleasant odors. Higher acids are wax-like solids, practically odorless.
    
- **Boiling Points:**
    - Higher than aldehydes, ketones, and even alcohols of comparable molecular mass.
        
    - Due to extensive intermolecular hydrogen bonding (exist as dimers in vapor phase or aprotic solvents).
        
- **Solubility:**
    - Simple aliphatic acids (up to 4 carbons) miscible with water (H-bonding with water).
        
    - Solubility decreases with increasing carbon atoms (hydrophobic interaction).
        
    - Benzoic acid (aromatic) nearly insoluble in cold water.
        
    - Soluble in less polar organic solvents.
