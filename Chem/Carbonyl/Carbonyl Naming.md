1. Aldehydes and Ketones
- **Common Names:**
    - **Aldehydes:** Derived from corresponding carboxylic acids by replacing "-ic acid" with "-aldehyde". Location of substituents by Greek letters (α, β, γ, δ) from carbon directly linked to aldehyde group.
        
    - **Ketones:** Name two alkyl/aryl groups bonded to carbonyl group. Locations of substituents by Greek letters (α, α', β, β'). Acetone (dimethyl ketone). Alkyl phenyl ketones named as "phenone" (e.g., acetophenone).
        
- **IUPAC Names:**
    - **Aldehydes:** "Alkanal" (replace 'e' of alkane with 'al'). Number longest chain from aldehyde carbon (C-1). If aldehyde group attached to ring, suffix "carbaldehyde" (e.g., cyclohexanecarbaldehyde). Benzaldehyde is accepted.
        
    - **Ketones:** "Alkanone" (replace 'e' of alkane with 'one'). Number from end nearer to carbonyl group. Carbonyl carbon is C-1 in cyclic ketones.
        
	    - **Multiple Aldehyde Groups:** If more than two, name parent chain and add "tricarbaldehyde" (e.g., propane-1,2,3-tricarbaldehyde).
	
	2. Carboxylic acids
		- **Common Names:** Derived from natural sources, end with "-ic acid" (e.g., formic acid from ants, acetic acid from vinegar).
		
		- **IUPAC Names:**
	    - Aliphatic: "Alkanoic acid" (replace 'e' of alkane with 'oic acid'). Carboxylic carbon is C-1.
			
	    - Multiple carboxyl groups: Name alkyl chain, add multiplicative prefix (di/tri/etc.) and "carboxylic acid" (e.g., ethanedioic acid for oxalic acid, propane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid).
			
	    - Aromatic: Benzoic acid (benzenecarboxylic acid).
