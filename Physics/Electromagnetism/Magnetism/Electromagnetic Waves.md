## [[Displacement Current]]:
- Produces a Magnetic Field like Conduction Current. Ensures consistency in electromagnetic theory and explains EM wave generations
## Regions of Current in Capacitor
- Outside: I_c$
- Inside: $I_d$ during charge/discharge
- Total: $I_d + I_c$
> $I_d$ is present in vaccum/insulating medium if Electric field is changing
## Sources:
- Requirements: Accelerated charges radiate EM waves
- Not Sources:
	- Stationary charges: only Electrostatic
	- Charges in Uniform Motion: only Magnetic
- Mechanism: An oscillating charge produces an oscillating electric field which in turn makes an oscillating magnetic field. These fields regenerate each other propagating an EM wave
- Frequency: Frequency of EM wave = Frequency of oscillating charge
- Energy Source: Energy of propagating wave comes from accelerated charge
## Nature:
- Transverse Nature:E, B, and direction of propagation are $\perp$ to each other
- **Sinusoidal Variation:** E and B fields vary sinusoidally in space and time.
	- $E = E_0 \sin(kz - \omega t)$
	- $B = B_0 \sin(kz - \omega t)$
	> Where $k = 2\pi/\lambda$ (wave number), $\omega = 2\pi\nu$ (angular frequency).

- **Speed of Propagation (in vacuum):** $\displaystyle c = \frac{\omega}{k} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$.
- **Relationship between E and B magnitudes:** $\displaystyle B_0 = \frac{E_0}{c}$.
- **Medium Dependence:** In a material medium with permittivity $\epsilon$ and magnetic permeability $\mu$, the speed of light is $\displaystyle v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu \epsilon}}$.
- **No Material Medium Required:** EM waves are self-sustaining oscillations of fields and can propagate through a vacuum. The "ether" hypothesis was disproven by experiments like Michelson-Morley.
## Energy and Momentum:
- **Energy Density:** Associated with electric field $\displaystyle u_E = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_0 E^2$ and magnetic field $\displaystyle u_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0} B^2$
- **Radiation Pressure:** Due to momentum transfer. For complete absorption, momentum $\displaystyle p = \frac{U}{c}$, where $U$ is the total energy transferred.
