### Structure of Carbonyl Group:
- **Hybridization:** Carbonyl carbon is $sp^2$ hybridized.
- **Bonds:** 3 $\sigma$ (coplanar, ~120° bond angles) & 1 $\pi$ bond with O.
- **Polarity:** $\ce{C=O}$ bond is highly polarized (oxygen more electronegative). Carbonyl carbon is electrophilic, carbonyl oxygen is nucleophilic.
- **Resonance:** Neutral $\ce{C=O}$ and dipolar $\ce{C+-O-}$ structures contribute, giving substantial dipole moment.
### Aldehyde:
#### Carbonyl Group:
- Bonded to 1 R and 1 H
```smiles
[R]C(=O)
```
#### Uses:
- **Starting Materials/Reagents:** Acetic acid, ethyl acetate, vinyl acetate, polymers, drugs.
- **Formaldehyde:** Formalin (preservative), bakelite, urea-formaldehyde glues.
- **Acetaldehyde:** Acetic acid, ethyl acetate, polymers, drugs.
- **Benzaldehyde:** Perfumery, dye industries.
- **Odors/Flavors:** Butyraldehyde, vanillin, acetophenone, camphor
### Ketone:
#### Carbonyl Group:
- Bonded to 2 R
```smiles
[R]C([R])(=O)
```
#### Uses:
- solvents (acetone)
#### Physical Properties:
- **State:** Methanal (gas), ethanal (volatile liquid), others (liquid/solid).
    
- **Boiling Points:**
    - Higher than hydrocarbons and ethers of comparable molecular mass (due to dipole-dipole interactions).
    - Lower than alcohols of similar molecular mass (no intermolecular H-bonding).
	
- **Solubility:**
    - Lower members (methanal, ethanal, propanone) miscible with water (H-bonding with water).
    - Solubility decreases with increasing alkyl chain length (hydrophobic part).
    - Soluble in organic solvents.
	
- **Odour:** Lower aldehydes (sharp, pungent); larger molecules (less pungent, more fragrant). Used in perfumes/flavoring agents.
