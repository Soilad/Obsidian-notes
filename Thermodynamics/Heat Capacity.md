> Amount of [[Heat]] per unit temperature per mol
$\displaystyle C = \frac QT = \frac{dQ}{dT}$
## In [[Isobaric]] Process
$$
\begin{align}
Q = \Delta U + W\\
\implies dQ = dU + PdV\\
\implies dQ = dU + RdT\\
\implies \frac {dQ}{dT} = \frac{dU}{dT} + R\\
\implies C_p = \frac{dU}{dT} + R\\
\end{align}
$$
## In [[Isochoric]] Process
$$
\begin{align}
Q = \Delta U + W\\
\implies dQ = dU + PdV\\
\implies dQ = dU\\
\implies \frac {dQ}{dT} = \frac{dU}{dT}\\
\implies C_v = \frac{dU}{dT}\\
\end{align}
$$
## From Both ([[Mayer's Formula]])
$$
\begin{align}
C_p-C_v = R
\end{align}
$$
