> $Q = 0$
> $P, V$: Variables 
$$
\begin{align}
dU+dW=dQ=0\\
dW = PdV
dU = \frac f2 nRdT = \frac f2 d(PV)\\
\implies dU = \frac f2 (VdP + PdV)\\
\frac f2 (VdP)+\frac f2(PdV) = -PdV\\
\frac f2 (VdP) = -(1+\frac f2) PdV\\
\int \frac{dP}P = -\frac{1+\frac f2}{\frac f2} \int \frac{dV}V\\
\ln(\frac {P}{P_0}) = -\frac{1+\frac f2}{\frac f2} \ln(\frac{V}{V_0})\\
\end{align}
$$
#### From [[Heat Capacity Ratio]]
$$
\begin{align}
\implies \ln(\frac {P}{P_0}) = -\gamma \ln(\frac{V}{V_0})\\
\implies \frac {P}{P_0} = \frac{V}{V_0}^{-\gamma}\\
\implies \frac {P}{P_0}\frac{V}{V_0}^\gamma = 1\\
\implies P_0V_0^\gamma = PV^\gamma = constant\\
\implies \frac{nRT}VV^\gamma = constant\\
\implies TV^{\gamma-1} = constant\\
\end{align}
$$
